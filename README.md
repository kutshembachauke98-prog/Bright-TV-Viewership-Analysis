# Bright-TV-Viewership-Analysis
Analyzing TV viewership data to understand user behavior, content, preferences and demographic patterns
Description:

This project focuses on analysing TV viewership data to understand user behaviour, content preferences, and demographic patterns. The dataset includes information such as user ID, gender, age, province, viewing time, and channels watched.

The analysis was performed using Excel (Pivot Tables and Charts) and includes data cleaning, transformation, and visualization to generate meaningful insights.

📊 Objectives:
To analyse viewership trends across different channels
To understand audience distribution by gender, age, and province
To identify peak viewing times
To generate insights for content improvement
🛠 Tools Used:
Microsoft Excel
Databricks
Canva
Pivot Tables
Charts (Bar, Pie, Column)
--📈 Key Features:
Dataset overview (users, sessions, demographics)
Gender distribution analysis
Age group segmentation
Province-based viewership analysis
Time-of-day viewing patterns
Top 5 most watched channels

)
📈 Key Insights from the Analysis:
Male users dominate viewership, accounting for approximately 87% of total users, while female and unknown users make up a small proportion.
The majority of viewers fall within the youth and adult age groups, indicating that younger audiences are the most active users.
Sports and entertainment channels are the most watched, with SuperSport and major events ranking highest in engagement.
Viewership is highest in Gauteng province, followed by Western Cape, while provinces such as Northern Cape show the lowest engagement.
Users are most active during the afternoon and evening periods, suggesting peak viewing times during after-school and after-work hours.
A small percentage of records fall under unknown categories, indicating missing demographic information in the dataset.
